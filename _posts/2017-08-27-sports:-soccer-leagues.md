# The Leagues

Similarities between the leagues:
- Season stretches from August to May
- Most games played on weekends
- Bottom 3 teams of top tier move to second tier in the next season; top 3 of
  second tier move up ("promotion and relegation" system)

The following leagues are sorted by revenue by season
[[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_professional_sports_leagues_by_revenue)].

## Premier League (4,865 mil € per season)

**Country**: England

**Year Created**: 1992

**Teams in Top Tier**: 20

**Controlling teams**: Manchester United (13 titles), Chelsea (5), Arsenal (3),
Manchester City (2)

Teams play one another twice in the season, one home and one away, making up 38 
total matches per team. The matches make up two "legs", where the scoring for
each pair of matches is based on the "two-legged tie" scheme. As [Wikipedia puts
it](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-legged_tie), if we consider the two
matches:
- First leg: Team A 4--1 Team B
- Second leg: Team B 2--1 Team A
The aggregate score results in Team A 5--3 Team B, or Team A wins the tie.

## Bundesliga (4,865 mil € per season)

**Country**: Germany

**Year Created**: 1963

**Teams in Top Tier**: 18

**Controlling teams**: Bayern Munich (26 titles), Borussia Dortmund

## La Liga (2,437 mil € per season)

**Country**: Spain

**Year Created**: 1929

**Teams in Top Tier**: 20

**Controlling teams**: Real Madrid, FC Barcelona, Athletico Madrid

Since the start of the league, Real Madrid and FC Barcelona have claimed the
title 59 times (36 of last 50). Because of their dominance, the
match up of these squads is known as 'El Clasico', a highly anticipated event
[[2](https://www.onlinegamblingbible.com/betting-on-la-liga-introduction-to-spanish-football/)].
